By mid-2026, more than a dozen US states have passed laws requiring age verification on websites and apps that serve adult content. The rules vary state by state — here's the current picture and what it means for Android users.

## States With Active Age-Verification Laws

| State | Law | Status |

## What This Means for App Users

Most laws apply to **websites**, not Android apps directly — but app publishers often add their own verification when an app's web counterpart is regulated, since the same user base spans both. In practice that means:

- Government-ID or credit-card check on first launch in regulated states
- VPN detection on some apps (use disclosed)
- Some apps geo-block regulated states entirely instead of implementing verification

## How to Stay Compliant as a User

- **Don't fake your location** to bypass a check — most apps log mismatches and ban accounts
- Use the app's official verification flow even if it's tedious; alternatives often involve handing your ID to a sketchy third party
- If an app you used to use is now blocked in your state, check the publisher's site for a verified workaround (most have FAQs)

## What's Next

Several states have age-verification bills moving through legislatures in 2026, including Ohio, Pennsylvania, and Georgia. Federal-level proposals exist but none have passed.

For app developers, the patchwork means building per-state logic — and for users it means the same app may behave very differently depending on which state you're in.
