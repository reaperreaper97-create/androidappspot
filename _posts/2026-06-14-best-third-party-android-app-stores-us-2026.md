When an app isn't on Google Play — geo-blocked, removed for policy reasons, or never published there — these third-party Android stores cover most gaps. All four are legal and free to use in the US.

## Aptoide

- **Strength:** Massive catalog including apps removed from Play. User-uploaded but moderated.
- **Weakness:** Quality varies; check uploader reputation and recent reviews.
- **Best for:** Apps removed from Play that the original developer also publishes here.

## APKMirror

- **Strength:** Strictly verified APKs only — every upload is signature-checked against the original publisher.
- **Weakness:** No actual store app on Android; you download APKs via mobile browser.
- **Best for:** Grabbing older versions of mainstream apps when a new release breaks something.

## F-Droid

- **Strength:** Open-source apps only, built from source by F-Droid maintainers. No tracking.
- **Weakness:** Smaller catalog; no commercial apps.
- **Best for:** Privacy-focused users and FOSS replacements for Google apps.

## Amazon Appstore

- **Strength:** Curated catalog, Amazon Coins rewards on some apps, runs Android apps on Windows 11 via WSA.
- **Weakness:** Limited selection vs Play; some apps haven't updated in months.
- **Best for:** Fire tablet owners and casual users who already have an Amazon account.

## How to Pick

| Need | Recommended Store |
|---|---|
| App removed from Play | Aptoide → developer's site |
| Older version of mainstream app | APKMirror |
| Privacy/FOSS replacement | F-Droid |
| Casual everyday apps | Amazon Appstore |

For most users in the US, having APKMirror bookmarked plus one general-purpose store (Aptoide or Amazon) covers nearly every use case where Google Play falls short.
